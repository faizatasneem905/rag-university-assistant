# Academic Operations Manual

## 1. Credit System

Each course carries a defined number of credit hours. Students must complete 120 total credits to graduate.

A standard full-time semester load is 15 credit hours.

Students may register for up to 18 credit hours with departmental approval.

---

## 2. Probation Monitoring

Students placed on Academic Probation are required to schedule a meeting with their academic advisor within the first three weeks of the semester to discuss performance improvement.

Failure to meet with an advisor may result in registration restrictions or holds on course enrollment.

---

## 3. Suspension Reinstatement

Students returning from Academic Suspension must:

- Submit a reinstatement request
- Provide a study improvement plan
- Achieve a semester GPA of at least 2.5

Failure to meet performance expectations after reinstatement may result in permanent dismissal.

---

## 4. Internship Requirement

Some programs require completion of a supervised internship before graduation.

Internship completion must be documented and approved by the department coordinator.

---

## 5. Examination Eligibility

Only students meeting attendance and financial clearance requirements may sit final examinations.

Students with outstanding dues may have examination access blocked.

---

## 6. Misconduct Review Procedure

All academic misconduct cases are documented formally.

Students are given an opportunity to respond before penalties are applied.

Penalties are recorded in the student disciplinary file.
